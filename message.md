Goal
Create a system where all goods include a CO₂-equivalent fee across the full supply chain.


Purpose: change behavior through price (not awareness).


High-emission products become expensive → people buy lower-emission alternatives.


Core design questions you explored
1. Do we have the data?
Yes — lifecycle assessments (LCAs) / product carbon footprints already exist.


They include extraction → production → transport → use → disposal.


2. Where should the fee be applied?
Option discussed: charge at every stage (upstream pricing).


Oil extraction → pays for extraction emissions


Manufacturer → pays for production


Retail/logistics → pays for distribution


Disposal: likely via extended producer responsibility (manufacturer pays).


End result: total cost accumulates into final price.


3. EU / Denmark feasibility
Hard to implement fully at national level due to EU single market rules.


Likely needs:


EU-wide implementation, or


Carefully designed national taxes that don’t distort trade.


4. What to do with the revenue
You rejected:


Subsidies for companies to “go green”


You proposed:


Public good spending


Minimum income / welfare to offset higher prices


Principle: don’t protect polluters; protect people.


5. How to set the price per CO₂
Based on “social cost of carbon”


Rough ranges: tens to hundreds per ton


Trade-off:


Higher price → stronger behavioral change


But requires stronger social redistribution


6. Example: beef pricing
~30 kg CO₂e per kg of beef (lifecycle)


At $100/ton → ~€3 extra per kg


Insight: smaller than expected unless carbon price is high


7. What’s included in that footprint
Yes, it includes:


Feed production (including oil-based fertilizers)


Methane from cows


Land use


Processing


8. Oil’s role
~400 kg CO₂e per barrel (lifecycle)


Oil price would rise significantly under the system


But:


Oil is only a fraction of beef’s footprint


Most emissions come from biology (methane), not oil


9. Key realization
Even large upstream costs (like oil) dilute across products


Biggest impact comes from:


High-emission processes (e.g. livestock)


Not just industrial inputs


10. Software / digital products
Indirect emissions:


Hardware manufacturing


Electricity (data centers, devices)


Carbon pricing would show up in:


Hardware costs


Cloud/service pricing

Underlying open questions (still unresolved)
Exact carbon price needed to meaningfully shift behavior


How to enforce accurate lifecycle accounting globally


How to handle imports from non-compliant countries


Political feasibility within EU structures


Transition speed vs. social stability

→ Turn environmental impact into unavoidable economic pressure, then redistribute the money to keep society stable while forcing the market to decarbonize.
